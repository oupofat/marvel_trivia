Phil Coulson was a director of the S.H.I.E.L.D. He worked alongside Nick Fury, joining him on many different secret missions. He was held responsible for the union of the Avengers after Loki arrived in search of the tesseract. He was a huge fan of Captain America – and when Steve was brought onto the helicarrier Phil showed him his vintage Captain America card collection. Phil gave his life during the attack on the helicarrier to give the Avengers a purpose and a reason to fight together.

The Aether is one of the six Infinity Stones. It is “awakened” by Jane Foster after she is investigating a strange anomaly. The Aether has the ability to transform reality and is extremely powerful. It consumed Jane when she touched it and nearly killed her. At one point, she was able to project energy when someone grabbed her arm. By the end of the film, she was able to successful rid her body of the Aether and it was properly contained.

During Wade Wilson’s mercenary days, he spent most of his nights at the “Sister Margaret’s School for Wayward Children” where his best friend is the bartender. Although Wade is a very strong and dangerous mercenary, his best friend is quite the opposite, which is surprisingly with all of the men and women he deals with. His best friend is the only one from his previous life that knows Wade has taken on the alter ego “Deadpool” – although he often makes fun of his horrendous appearance.



There are six Infinity Stones in total – each of them have their own specific abilities which predates mankind. Only the most powerful beings in all of the realms are capable of wielding their power. One of the Infinity Stones was recovered from Loki’s staff which had found its way into the hands of many – including S.H.I.E.L.D and Strucker and then the Avengers. Ultron was the only one who managed to free the Infinity Stone, using it to give Vision life.
